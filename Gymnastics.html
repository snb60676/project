Gymnastics

Gymnastics is a sport that requires not only balance but also strength,flexibility,agility, endurance and coordination.

Gymnastics can be done competitively but also for fun! Involving four different events including; Vault, Bars, Beam, Floor. Competitive Gymnastics consist of 10 different leveland is designed where each level adds harder skills and longer routines. Competition season is held in either the fall or the spring, depending on you level and gymnastics typically compete in 5-7 competitons each season excluding things like; State, Regionals and Nationals.

Competitive gymnastics consisit of Levels 1-10.

Level 1-5 is considered compulary and everyone gymnast is required to perform the same routines on each and every event. These gymnast compete at competitions chosen by the coaches and owner of the gym, and also have the oportunity to qualiy for State Meet.

Level 6-10 is considered optional and gymnast and coaches design and create the routines but are required to have certain elements in each routine. Optionals also compete in competitions also chosen by their owners/coaches, but they also cann qualify to state, and then from there can qualify to regionals, and then from they can qualify to nationals.

Gymnast also have to achieve certain requirements to move on to the next level. Although it is quite normal for gymnast to repeat levels it is also normal for some very talented gymnast to test out of levels and never actually compete that level because they were to good for it.

Once a gymnast who is very serious about gymnastics reaches age 10-12 they have to decide if they want to go Elite or go to college route. Choosing to go elite will make them focus on training for the Olympics and/or move away to train at more advanced training facilities. Simone Biles

Simone Biles can easily be recognized as the best Gymnast in American History. She started Gymnastics at age 6 and hasnt stopped since. At the 2016 Olympic Games in Rio(her first apperence in the Olympics) she set an American record for the most gold medals in women's gymnastics at a single games. She also equally matched many other different records with her medals won in Rio. She also became the 6th female gymnast in history to win an individual gold medal in the World Championship and the Olympic Games. She and another teammate Gabby Douglas are the only American females to win both the individual all-around gold medal and team gold medal in the same Olympic games. Simone, now 21 years old, again winning all four events at the World Cahmpionship this year is preparing to head back to the Olympics in 2020 where her goal and only true competition is to beat herself.
